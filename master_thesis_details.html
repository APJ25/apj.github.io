Magnetic Optimization of Reconfigurable Phase-shift Full Bridge Converter

Master's Thesis Project (2023 - 2025)

This thesis focuses on the advanced **magnetic optimization** of **reconfigurable Phase-shift Full Bridge (PSFB) converters**, specifically for demanding **railway applications**. The primary goal is to enhance efficiency, reduce power losses, and minimize the physical footprint of magnetic components by implementing novel design strategies and control techniques.

Our approach involves detailed modeling and simulation of various transformer and inductor core materials and winding configurations. We aim to identify the optimal balance between power density, thermal performance, and cost, while ensuring robust operation under varying load and input conditions typical in railway power systems.

Key aspects of the research include the development of a comprehensive **power loss analysis framework** that accounts for both switching and conduction losses, as well as core and winding losses in magnetic components. Furthermore, we are exploring adaptive control algorithms that allow the PSFB converter to reconfigure its operating parameters dynamically, optimizing performance across a wide range of power demands.
