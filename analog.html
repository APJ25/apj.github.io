Introduction

Processing a PN diode in a cleanroom involves a series of steps to fabricate the diode with precision and reliability. Cleanrooms provide an environment where there are very few particles in the air. This room is equipped with yellow lighting, to minimize the exposure of photoresist materials to certain wavelengths of light, particularly ultraviolet (UV) light. The specialty of the lithographical technique used in the PN junction processing is photolithography. Photolithography is a fundamental process in semiconductor fabrication, and its specialty lies in its ability to precisely define patterns on a wafer surface with high resolution. Resolution in photolithography refers to the ability of the technique to reproduce fine details and patterns accurately on the wafer surface. To determine the resolution in the below described PN junction processing, specific information about the wavelength of the UV light source, the type of photomask, and the characteristics of the photoresist would be necessary. The quality of the lithography patterns after exposure was evaluated using microscopy. Radon Sensing Circuit Design

Radon sensing consists of a detector, preamplifier, pulse shaping amplifier, and a peak detector. Instead of a physical detector, the current pulse from a radon decay event was simulated.

Simulations

Each stage of the circuit was simulated in LTspice to verify the design calculations before implementation.

Circuit Implementation & Measurements

The circuits were assembled on a breadboard. The output of each stage was verified with an oscilloscope, confirming that the physical implementation matched the simulated results.
